 About us

We are a group of four second year Bioinformatics students at the HAN University of Applied Sciences, working on a project with the aim of investigating the health benefits of yam and bitter gourd compounds.

 The project

Yam (Dioscorea) and bitter gourd (Momordica charantia) are claimed to have positive effects on i.a. sugar levels, skin, night vision, the immune system and menopause. In order to find out if these claims are true, scientific research is necessary. Therefore, it is important to first consider whether a link can be found between the characteristics of the plant and the possible consequences for the health of the consumer in the existing literature.


To manually read all the online articles and to find all of the connections by yourself would be a very repetetive and time-consuming task. Hence, we came up with the plan to develop an application that will visualize the links between the compounds and health effects from the articles after an automated text mining process. That is how this web application came into being.


In addition to the literature research via text mining, the health effects of yam and bitter gourd compounds are currently being researched on Caenorhabditis elegans, a nematode which is extensively used as a model organism. By feeding the worms with yam and bitter gourd and observing their response, we can learn more about the health effects of these vegetables.
